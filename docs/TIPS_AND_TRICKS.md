# 📘 Menu Planner – Power User Tips & Tricks

A collection of smart shortcuts, hidden behaviours and pro moves to get the absolute most out of Menu Planner.

---

## 🍽️ Menu Generation

### Get Better Variety Every Week
Select **4-6 categories** when generating instead of just 1-2. The more categories, the more diverse your week becomes. A week with only "Chicken" selected means 6 chicken dinners — probably not what you want!

### The Sweet Spot for Category Size
Each category should ideally have **10-15+ recipes**. Too few = repeats every week. Too many = some recipes never get picked. Aim for a balanced collection across all categories.

### Use Regenerate Freely
Not happy with Monday's dinner? Just hit **Regenerate** — it's instant and free. The deduplication system remembers recently served recipes, so regenerating a few times in a row still gives you fresh options.

### Favourites as a Comfort Week
Had a rough week? Select **only Favourites** when generating for a week of guaranteed crowd-pleasers. Star your best recipes and they're always one click away.

### Mix Favourites with Categories
You can select **Favourites AND other categories** at the same time. This creates a pool that includes your starred recipes plus everything in the other selected categories — great for a week that has some sure wins and some variety.

## 📂 Categories

### Rename to Match How You Actually Think
The default categories are generic. Rename them to fit your family's language. "Ground Meat & Sausages" might become "Kjøttdeig" in your household. The internal code never changes, so renaming is always safe.

### Create a "Weekend" Category
Add a category called **Weekend** (or Helgemiddag) for recipes that take longer or need more effort — Lasagne, Slow Cooker, BBQ. Then on weekdays select quick categories, and on weeks where you have time, include Weekend.

### Use "Uncategorized" as a Holding Area
When you add a recipe and aren't sure which category fits, leave it in Uncategorized. Periodically review Uncategorized and move recipes to proper categories. A clean Uncategorized = a well-organised recipe collection.

### Merge Duplicates Before They Multiply
If you end up with "Pasta" and "Pasta & Noodles" as separate categories, use **Merge into...** to combine them immediately. The longer you wait, the more recipes end up split across both.

## 🥗 Recipes

### Import First, Customise Second
Import a recipe pack to get a solid base, then **edit individual recipes** to match how your family actually makes them. Change ingredients, swap quantities, add your own tips in the comment field.

### The Comment Field is Your Friend
Use the **Comment** field on each recipe for family notes:
- "Kids don't like the onion — leave it out"
- "Use 600g meat instead of 500g for leftovers"
- "Grandma's version — double the garlic"

These notes show up on the recipe detail page as a reminder every time you cook it.

### Add Time Honestly
If a recipe says 20 min but actually takes you 45 min, **edit the time**. The shopping list and menu view show these times — accurate times make planning your evenings much easier.

### Keep Difficulty Accurate
Set difficulty honestly. If a recipe is marked Easy but actually stresses you out, change it to Medium. The generator doesn't filter by difficulty, but it helps you quickly scan the week and spot if Thursday is going to be a nightmare.

### Bulk Cleanup via All Recipes
Go to **All Recipes** and sort/filter to find:
- Recipes with no category (Uncategorized)
- Recipes you imported but never actually cook
- Duplicates from different packs

Regular cleanup = a better menu generator result.

## 🧅 Pantry (Basisvarer)

### Do One Big Pantry Setup First
Spend 5 minutes going through the default staples list and removing things your household genuinely never has. A pantry that reflects reality is much more useful than a bloated default list.

### Add Household-Specific Staples
After resetting to defaults, add the things your family *always* has that aren't in the default list. Common ones people forget:
- Specific spice blends you always have
- Condiments (soy sauce, fish sauce, hot sauce)
- Tinned goods you stock regularly (coconut milk, crushed tomatoes)

### Check the Shopping List After Generating
After generating a menu, go to **Shopping List** immediately. Anything greyed out = you already have it. Scan for anything that shouldn't be greyed out (maybe your pantry list is too generous) and adjust.

### The Language Trick
The pantry stores items in both Norwegian and English. If you switch the app language from English to Norwegian, your pantry view changes to show only Norwegian items. Use this if different family members prefer different languages — each person sees the pantry in their own language.

## 👨‍👩‍👧 Household & Profiles

### Set Up Profiles for Everyone
Even if family members share a login, give everyone their own **profile**. This means:
- The activity log shows who added what
- Profile switching is one tap (with optional PIN)
- Kids can use the app without accidentally changing settings

### Use the PIN for Shared Devices
Set a 4-digit **PIN** under Settings. The PIN lets anyone switch back to the Owner profile quickly without typing the full password. Perfect for a tablet or family computer.

### Editor Role for Cooking Partners
Give your spouse or partner **Editor** role so they can add recipes and regenerate the menu. Keep Viewer for kids who just want to see what's for dinner.

## 🛒 Shopping List

### Check it Right Before Shopping
The shopping list reflects the current weekly menu. If you regenerate the menu, the shopping list updates. Always check the shopping list **after** your final menu decision, not before.

### Use it on Your Phone
The app is mobile-friendly. Pull it up in the supermarket, tick off items mentally as you go, and use the pantry greying to skip what you already have.

### Plan for Leftovers
If a recipe serves 4 but you're cooking for 6, remember to mentally double quantities. The shopping list shows exact ingredient amounts from the recipe — adjust in your head or edit the recipe quantities directly.

## ⚙️ Settings & Admin

### Activity Log as a Family Diary
The activity log isn't just for debugging — it's a fun record of your household's cooking history. "Added Taco Fredag" on a Friday, "Regenerated menu" every Sunday morning — you can see patterns in how your family uses the app.

### Delete Unused Recipe Packs
If you imported a pack and only use 2 out of 20 recipes, consider removing the pack and **manually re-adding** just those 2 recipes to your household. A leaner recipe database = faster, better menu generation.

### Keep Categories Below 12
More than 12 categories makes the menu generator screen crowded and harder to use. If you have more, merge related ones. Quality over quantity.

## 🌍 Language Tips

### Norwegian = Better for Norwegian Recipes
Switch to **Norwegian** when browsing Norwegian recipe packs — the titles and instructions read more naturally, and the category names match what you'd call them in the kitchen.

### English for International Recipes
When importing international packs (Italian, Mexican, Asian), keep the app in **English** for those sessions — the ingredient names and instructions are written with English in mind.

### Both Languages Work for Menu Planning
The menu home page, shopping list and pantry all work great in either language. Pick whichever feels natural and stick with it.

## 🚀 Pro Workflows

### The "Sunday Reset" Routine
Every Sunday:
1. Open Menu Planner
2. Click Regenerate
3. Select your categories for the week
4. Generate until you like the week
5. Open Shopping List
6. Go shopping

Takes under 5 minutes once you're set up properly.

### The "New Season" Refresh
4 times a year (spring, summer, autumn, winter):
1. Review your categories — add seasonal ones (Grill for summer, Supper for winter)
2. Import any new recipe packs
3. Remove recipes you didn't cook all season
4. Update your pantry with seasonal staples

### The "Crowd Pleaser" Fallback
Create a category called **Safe Dinners** with 8-10 recipes the whole family loves without complaint. When you're tired, just generate from that category only. No debates, no "I don't like this" — guaranteed peace at the dinner table.
